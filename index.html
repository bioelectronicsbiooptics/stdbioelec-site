Research themes

The lab connects electric-field control, DNA chemistry, nanomaterials, and microfluidics into practical bioelectronic platforms.

DNA data storage

Random access, molecular encoding, DNA data ink, and processing-in-memory workflows for digital preservation.

Electric-field biochips

Electrode arrays, dielectrophoretic assembly, and field-guided synthesis for programmable molecular operations.

Bio-optical interfaces

Fluorescent DNA memory, optical readout, and biochip visualization for data and diagnostics workflows.

Soft bioelectronics

CNT composites, liquid metal electrodes, flexible sensors, and wearable interfaces for biological measurement.
